\section{Quantum note}
\label{sec:quantum}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A few-minutes memory- and depth-hard $\Hhard$ per element also defeats
Grover~\cite{grover1996}: a quantum search over an $n$-bit space needs
$\sim 2^{n/2}$ \emph{coherent} evaluations of the oracle, and each such evaluation
is an hours-scale, deeply sequential Argon2d computation --- coherence would have to
survive the full \emph{depth} of every one, which is impractical. Hence sub-256-bit
standards (96 or 128 bits per stage) are quantum-defensible \emph{without} a 256-bit
seed --- the memory- and depth-hardness that buys the $\sftstar$ floor classically
also caps the quantum speed-up.
