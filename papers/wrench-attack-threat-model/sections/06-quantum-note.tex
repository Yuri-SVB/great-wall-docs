\section{Quantum note}
\label{sec:quantum}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Memory- and depth-hardness is a tunable lever, and even its modest settings
suffice here. A quantum search over an $n$-bit space needs $\sim 2^{n/2}$
\emph{coherent} evaluations of the oracle~\cite{grover1996}, and each such
evaluation is a deeply sequential Argon2d computation whose full \emph{depth} the
coherence must survive --- a cost the quantum resource-estimation literature
already finds decisive even against oracles far cheaper than
ours~\cite{grassl-grover-aes}. Even a \emph{few-minutes} $\Hhard$ per element
therefore places Grover out of reach; an \emph{hours-scale} $\Hhard$ does so
\emph{a fortiori}. Hence sub-256-bit standards (96 or 128 bits per stage) are
quantum-defensible \emph{without} a 256-bit seed --- the memory- and
depth-hardness behind the floor's \emph{hard} bound ($\sftstar$) classically also caps the quantum
speed-up.

\begin{remark}[The one bound Grover does dent: the live-$o_N$ residual]
The duality's \emph{cheap} bound is a different matter. The floor also clears
$\sftstar$ via a cheap $2^{96}$ ledger brute of $\Sh_N$ (Stipulation~\ref{ass:feas},
\ref{s:infeasiblec}), and that cheap side inherits \emph{no} depth-hardness to guard
it: Grover halves it outright to $\sim 2^{48}$. Concretely, a Quantum WDY attacker
landing on an imminently- or currently-live final board $o_N$ --- the same coincidence
that opens the tolerated residual window (\S\ref{sec:residual-seizure}) --- faces only the cheap
$2^{96}$ brute of the terminal secret, and Grover reduces that to a
$\sim 2^{48}$ (``$48$-qubit'') floor.\footnote{Each Grover attempt is one cheap
evaluation of the terminal step --- a single $\Hcheap(o_N \,\|\, \Sh_N)$
key-derivation followed by the ledger decryption/spend check --- and \emph{nothing}
more: the search is over the $96$-bit $\Sh_N$ \emph{directly}, so Shamir
reconstruction (the holder's step of interpolating $\Sh_N$ from $r_N$ recognized
points) never enters the oracle loop. One quantum attempt is thus KDF $+$
decryption, not Shamir $+$ KDF $+$ decryption.} This touches nothing but the
residual: it does \emph{not} reach the memory-hard $\sftstar$ bound (guarded above),
and it applies only inside the already-$\varepsilon$-bounded live-$o_N$ sliver, whose
incentive-safety argument (\S\ref{sec:residual-dr}) turns on the kill economics, not on
the brute's width --- a $2^{48}$ Grover search buys $\Attacker$ nothing a classical
$2^{32}$ brute in that same window does not already grant.
\end{remark}
