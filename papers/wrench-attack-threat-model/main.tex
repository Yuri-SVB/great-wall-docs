\begin{abstract}
\noindent This paper contributes a \emph{design bar} for coercion-resistant
custody and a \emph{construction} that meets it, motivated by a sharp negative
result: custody designs that lack such a bar can be \emph{worse than useless},
actively endangering the holder they claim to protect. Physical-coercion
(``wrench'') attacks defeat custody schemes that key management and cryptography
treat as out of scope. We give a \emph{formal model} of coercion against a
secret-holder and prove that a large class of
``graceful'' recovery designs --- those that leave a device seizure holding a
\emph{feasible-but-slower} path to the secret --- are strictly worse than
useless: they manufacture a material incentive to \emph{assassinate} the
victim. We name this the \textbf{Deadly Race}, derive it from a single
impossibility (one cannot prove one has forgotten), and distil a design bar ---
the \textbf{No-Gray-Area Criterion} --- that any coercion-resistant scheme must
clear. We then present a construction --- the \textbf{Time-Gated Perceptual
Oracle} (TGPO) --- that clears the bar for the Bitcoin self-custody setting
while keeping custody \emph{individual},
and we locate a shipping alternative --- timelock-rescue vaults --- precisely:
it clears the same bar by the \emph{other} available route, at the cost of a
trusted third party. The framework fixes a bar; it does not claim TGPO is
the unique construction that clears it.
\end{abstract}
